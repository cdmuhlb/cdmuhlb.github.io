Research

I previously worked in the Simulating eXtreme Spacetimes collaboration, advised by Prof. Saul A. Teukolsky.

Here you will find descriptions of academic and research work that I have conducted over the years.

Graduate work

Cornell University 2008–2014

  • Magnetic effects on the low-T/W instability in differentially rotating neutron stars [Phys. Rev. D 90, 104014 / arXiv:1405.2144, APR13 abstract / APR13 slides]
  • Determination of stellar color temperature from 2MASS data [Class. Quantum Grav. 32, 065002 / arXiv:1410.7775 (acknowledgement)]
  • Effects of numerical flux reconstruction on the development of global fluid instabilities
  • Implemented an upwind constrained transport scheme for evolving the GRMHD equations
  • Optimized and refactored hydrodynamic portions of the Spectral Einstein Code, enabling investigations with additional microphysics [ApJ, 776, 47 / arXiv:1304.3384]
  • Implemented Zernike polynomial basis functions for B2 and B3 topologies [poster]
  • Implemented an initial data solver for irrotational binary neutron systems [Phys. Rev. D 93, 124062 / arXiv:1604.00782]

Other coauthored papers

  • Initial data for black hole-neutron star binaries, with rotating stars [arXiv:1607.07962]
  • Gravitational waveforms for neutron star binaries from binary black hole simulations [Phys. Rev. D 93, 044064 / arXiv:1509.05782]
  • Binary Neutron Stars with Arbitrary Spins in Numerical Relativity [Phys. Rev. D 92, 124012 / arXiv:1508.06986]
